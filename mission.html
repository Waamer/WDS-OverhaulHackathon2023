The Mission

Stephanie Wong's mission with InkLink is rooted in her personal journey of overcoming language barriers as an immigrant student in Canada. Fueled by her tech-savvy entrepreneurship, Stephanie founded InkLink with the vision of revolutionizing learning accessibility. InkLink, more than just a note-taking app, stands out for its commitment to inclusivity. Stephanie's dedication is evident in the app's features, designed to empower two key user groups: students grappling with language barriers and those facing challenges in taking traditional notes due to disabilities. These diverse users are collectively known as "Inklings." InkLink goes beyond conventional note-taking, offering innovative solutions such as speech-to-text functions, screen readers, text-to-speech capabilities, language translations, and captioning. Stephanie envisions a warm, inviting website with a simple design, prioritizing accessibility considerations like alternative texts, resizable fonts, high contrast, and zoom capabilities. As Canada's immigrant population grows, so does the potential impact of InkLink, bridging language gaps for newcomers. Additionally, the app addresses the needs of students with disabilities, providing a centralized platform for seamless note-sharing. Stephanie's mission is an inspiring journey toward a more accessible world of learning, and with InkLink, she aspires to redefine how education accommodates every learner.
